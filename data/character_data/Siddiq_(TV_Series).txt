Quick Answers














Who brought Siddiq to the Alexandria Safe Zone in The Walking Dead?









						In The Walking Dead, Carl is the one who brings Siddiq to the Alexandria Safe Zone. After encountering Siddiq and conducting Rick's three screening questions, Carl determines Siddiq's suitability for their community. Despite encountering Walkers en route, they successfully eliminate them and proceed to Alexandria.
					



						Provided by: Fandom
					












What role did Siddiq play in the Alexandria Safe Zone community after the war?









						In the post-war period, Siddiq became a key figure in the Alexandria Safe-Zone, contributing to construction projects within the community. He led a construction crew, including Wesley, an accomplished rider in Alexandria. Siddiq's crew played a crucial role in the community's safety, diverting herds away from the Safe-Zone as needed.
					



						Provided by: Fandom
					












Who does Siddiq have a romantic relationship with in The Walking Dead?









						Siddiq, a character from The Walking Dead TV series, is romantically involved with Rosita Espinosa. He believes that Rosita shares his feelings and will choose him over Eugene, although she does not feel the same way.
					



						Provided by: Fandom
					












What significant event did Siddiq witness in The Walking Dead?









						In The Walking Dead, Siddiq witnessed the death of Carl, who was aiding him in killing walkers, a ritual from Siddiq's mother to free their souls. He later participated in the final battle against the Saviors as part of the Militia. After Rick injured Negan, Siddiq was commanded to save him, signifying the termination of Negan's reign.
					



						Provided by: Fandom
					












How does Siddiq's character evolve over the years in The Walking Dead?









						Siddiq, from The Walking Dead, transitions from a lone survivor to an integral part of the Alexandria Safe-Zone. He is a compassionate individual who respects life, influenced by his mother's teachings. As a Muslim, he can recite Qur'an scripture. Before the apocalypse, he was an emergency resident and after his parents succumbed to walkers, he kills walkers to free their souls and honor his mother. He carries guilt over Carl's death, who was bitten saving him, and aims to honor Carl. He serves as the Alexandria Safe-Zone's doctor and sits on the Alexandria Council.
					



						Provided by: Fandom
					














Overview
[
]


Siddiq is a kind and selfless man, who greatly values life. Due to his mother's teachings, he tries to kill every walker he comes across, as he believes the souls are of the deceased are trapped in the undead corpses. He also feels guilty over Carl's death, since Carl got bitten trying to save Siddiq, and does his best to honor him. Growing up in an Islamic household, Siddiq is a Muslim and can recite scripture from the Quran.

That night, Siddiq gazes out the window in his house when Dante arrives to comfort him. He explains that everything is going to be okay because Alexandria is a special place, before declaring that Siddiq is his friend. He then starts clicking his tongue, which triggers Siddiq back to the 
beheadings
 and makes him realize that Dante was the Whisperer forcing him to watch as his friends were killed. They soon start fighting in the room as Siddiq tries to reach for a machete. Dante tackles him to the ground and puts him in a chokehold. 
"I didn't want this! Not you! Not like this. Close your eyes... Close your eyes,"
 Dante tells Siddiq in sorrow as he chokes him harder until he falls dead.



"
The World Before
"
[
]


Siddiq appears alive briefly in Dante's flashback to when he first arrived in Alexandria and Siddiq performed a physical on him.

As Dante closes Siddiq's eyes and prepares to stab him in the head, they are interrupted by 
Rosita
. Though Dante lies about Siddiq's whereabouts, Rosita quickly grows suspicious and Dante attacks while Siddiq reanimates. Drawn by his daughter's cries, the reanimated Siddiq nearly attacks Coco. However, Rosita manages to overpower Dante and puts Siddiq down with Dante's knife to the head before he can hurt anybody.

The next day, Siddiq is buried in the Alexandria graveyard and a funeral is held by Gabriel who recites a passage in his memory as Rosita and the residents mourn his death.



"
What We Become
"
[
]


Siddiq appears in Michonne's hallucinations, where Siddiq tells her to picture the sun. Michonne is at first glad to see him, but he starts blaming her for his death, as well as the deaths of Rick and Carl. Siddiq then asks what will happen to his family with him gone. Michonne tearfully states that Siddiq is her family, but the latter replies "this is what it feels like to be your family".
